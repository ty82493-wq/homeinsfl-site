The Clermont market

Home insurance in Clermont, right now

If your renewal came in higher this year, you're not imagining it. Across Florida, rising reinsurance costs, carriers pulling out of the state, and roof and litigation pressures have pushed rates up sharply — and Lake County hasn't been spared. What you actually pay, though, still comes down to your house: its roof, its age, and which credits make it onto the policy. That's where shopping more than one carrier earns its keep.

Clermont is a little different from the coast. Sitting inland on central Florida's rolling hills and the Clermont Chain of Lakes, homes here face less storm-surge risk than Tampa or the Atlantic shoreline — but hurricanes that cross the peninsula still bring damaging wind, and central Florida sees some of the most frequent summer thunderstorms and lightning in the country. Newer subdivisions off Highway 27 and older homes near historic downtown carry very different risk profiles, and carriers price them very differently. Knowing which carriers like which kind of home is half the battle.

Why two homes on the same street pay different rates

Roof age & wind mitigation

Roof age is the single biggest factor in Florida. A wind-mitigation inspection can unlock significant credits — we make sure they're applied.

Hurricane wind exposure

Inland Clermont skips most surge risk, but peninsula-crossing storms still bring wind. Your hurricane deductible drives a big part of the price.

Lakes & flood zones

The Chain of Lakes and low-lying parcels mean some homes carry real flood risk. Standard policies exclude flood — we can quote it separately.

Sinkhole & ground cover

Central Florida sits in a sinkhole-prone belt. We'll explain catastrophic ground-collapse vs. full sinkhole coverage so you choose knowingly.

Possibly. Standard home policies don't cover flood, and Clermont's Chain of Lakes and low-lying areas mean some properties carry genuine flood risk even though the city is inland. We can quote flood separately so you're not caught out.
